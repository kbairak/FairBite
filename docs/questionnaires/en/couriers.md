# Couriers — Questionnaire

This questionnaire is in two parts. **Part 1** asks the questions that are most
critical for our financial planning — just 5 quick ones. **Part 2** goes deeper
into your experience and would be incredibly valuable, but you've already helped
a lot if you stop after Part 1.

## Part 1: Essential (5 questions — ~3 minutes)

1. Roughly, what do you earn per hour and per month (net, after deductions)?

2. How many deliveries do you typically complete in an hour? Does it vary a lot?

3. In a typical hour, how much time is spent riding vs waiting at the restaurant
   vs finding the customer's door / apartment? What's the typical delivery
   distance / radius?

4. What ongoing costs do you personally bear? Think: bike maintenance, fuel /
   electricity, phone data, equipment replacement. Are you covered by insurance
   if something happens — accident, injury, damage to the order?

5. What would a new platform need to offer you (pay, conditions, respect) for
   you to switch?

## Part 2: Helpful context (optional — ~10 minutes)

### Background

6. How long have you been working as a delivery courier?

7. Do you work full-time or part-time? Do you work for one platform or multiple?

8. What area / city do you primarily work in?

9. Did you do this kind of work before the apps existed (e.g., for a specific
    restaurant)?

### Compensation (continued)

10. How are you paid — per delivery, hourly, or a mix? What's the structure?

11. Do you get tips? Roughly how much do they add?

12. Are you employed (with a contract) or are you treated as a freelancer /
    self-employed?

13. Do you get paid for downtime between orders? For waiting at the restaurant?

14. How do bonuses or incentives work if the platform offers them?

### Working Conditions & Hours

15. How do you get assigned orders? Can you reject one? What happens if you do?

16. What are your typical shift hours? Can you choose your schedule?

17. What's the longest distance you're asked to cover?

18. Do you feel pressured to rush or work unsafely to meet time targets?

19. How does the platform handle meal breaks or rest periods?

### Efficiency & Route (continued)

20. When you arrive at a restaurant, is the food usually ready? How long do you
    typically wait?

21. Which restaurants are the slowest? Do you avoid certain ones?

22. Does the platform ever group multiple orders into one trip? How often?

23. How much does your deliveries-per-hour change between lunch, dinner, and
    slow afternoon hours?

24. Do you often deliver to apartments with no elevator? How much does that slow
    you down?

25. What's the most common reason a delivery takes longer than expected?

### Insurance & Safety (continued)

26. Have you ever had an accident while delivering? What happened? Who covered
    what?

27. Do you feel safe while working? What would make you feel safer?

### Equipment & Ongoing Costs (continued)

28. Do you use your own bike? What type (bicycle, scooter, car)?

29. Have you ever had to replace equipment due to wear or damage out of your own
    pocket?

### Incidents (continued)

30. Has a customer ever falsely complained (missing food, late delivery)? How
    was it resolved?

31. What happens if your phone breaks or runs out of battery mid-shift?

32. Have you ever been deactivated or threatened with deactivation? Why?

### Support

33. When something goes wrong on a delivery, who do you contact (dispatcher,
    in-app chat)? How responsive are they? Do you feel supported by the platform
    when you have a problem?

### Churn & Satisfaction

34. What's the best part of this job?

35. What's the worst part?

36. How long do most couriers stay? Why do people quit? If you could change one
    thing about how the platform treats couriers, what would it be?

### Cooperative Model

37. Would you prefer to be an employee with benefits and protections, or do you
    prefer flexibility?

38. How would you feel about being a member-owner of a delivery cooperative —
    having a vote on decisions and a share of profits?

39. What would a new platform need to offer you (pay, conditions, respect) for
    you to switch? Anything else it should know to treat couriers better?
