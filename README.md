### Decentralizing the Last-Mile Economy

## 1. The Problem: The "Enshittification" of Delivery

Modern delivery platforms have transitioned from market facilitators to **rent-seekers**. Having achieved market dominance, they now exploit all participants through:

* **Exorbitant Commissions:** Taking 25-35% from small businesses already operating on thin margins.
* **Algorithmic Oppression:** Using "black-box" logic to pressure couriers into unsafe or underpaid labor.
* **User Degradation:** Inflating prices through hidden service fees and data harvesting.
* **The Exit Trap:** Prioritizing shareholder returns over the sustainability of the local food ecosystem.

---

## 2. The Solution: Platform Cooperativism

**OurFood** is an Open Source, multi-stakeholder cooperative platform. It is not owned by venture capitalists, but by the people who produce, deliver, and consume the value.

### 2.1 The Triple-Stakeholder Model

Governance is split across three distinct member classes to ensure a permanent balance of power:

* **33% Merchants (The Producers):** Focused on sustainability, fair pricing, and direct customer relationships.
* **33% Riders (The Workforce):** Focused on fair wages, transparent dispatching, and workplace safety.
* **33% Consumers (The Community):** Focused on service quality, data privacy, and ethical consumption.

Within each member class, voting rights are spread equally.

---

## 3. Algorithmic Governance: "The Kill Switch"

Unlike traditional companies where a CEO is beholden only to a Board of Directors, OurFood introduces **Direct Algorithmic Accountability**.

### 3.1 The "Fire the CEO" Mechanism

Every interface (Merchant, Rider, and Consumer apps) features a "Revoke Confidence" function.

* **Activation:** If 25% of the weighted active membership triggers the function within a rolling 7-day window, a "Resolution Protocol" is initiated.
* **Resolution:** This triggers an automatic General Assembly via digital quorum. The CEO/Management must defend their roadmap, followed by a mandatory "Stay or Replace" vote within the app.
* **Proof of Participation:** Voting weight is not static; it is calculated via a decay function based on recent platform activity, ensuring that current users—not dormant accounts—shape the future.

---

## 4. Technology: The Digital Commons

We believe the software for a city's food infrastructure should be a **Public Good**.

* **Open Source (AGPL License):** The core protocol is transparent and auditable. Any city can "clone" the stack to start their own local node.
* **Data Sovereignty:** Merchants own their customer data. Riders own their performance history. The platform acts as a vault, not an owner.
* **Modular Architecture:** Designed to be lightweight. A "Node" can be deployed for a single neighborhood or an entire metropolis.

---

## 5. Economic Sustainability (The 10% Goal)

Our goal is to operate on the **minimum viable commission** (est. 8-12%). Because there are no external shareholders demanding 10x returns:

1. **No profits:** The platform does not make profits, by design. Courrier wages and development costs are subtracted from the total order volume and the rest is distributed in full to the merchants. As the software becomes stable and the platform spreads to more cities, development costs will shrink to near-zero.
2. **Fair courier wages**: Couriers should not belong to the precariat. They should enjoy fair wages, insurance, safety equipment, paid leave, reasonable hours and in general everything they need to lead a comfortable middle-class life.
3. **Local Circulation:** Capital stays within the city’s economy rather than being siphoned to offshore accounts.

---

## 6. Roadmap: The Path to Independence

1. **Phase 0 (Genesis):** Release of the Open Source core and the Multi-Stakeholder Manifesto.
2. **Phase 1 (The Sandbox):** Launch in a single neighborhood with 15-20 anchor merchants and a core group of worker-owners.
3. **Phase 2 (The Federation):** Inter-city collaboration. Shared R&D costs across different local cooperatives using the same protocol.
